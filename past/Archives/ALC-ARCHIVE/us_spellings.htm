Every Little Bit Helps

Every little bit helps.  That's one of our beliefs regarding the fight against the illogic of English spelling.  We are convinced that, in this era of mixed market share for periodicals, reader opinions matter to editors and publishers of newspapers and magazines.  These same editors and publishers update the stylistic aspects of their texts-- including spelling-- on a semi-annual basis.  If you like simplicity, and would like our English spelling to be a little bit simpler, then now is as good a time as any to

  1. print out both of the 'Simple Spelling' pages listed on the side of this screen, and

  2. cut/paste/edit in your word processor and send the following model letter-- with Simple Spelling pages attached-- to your favorite newspaper or magazine editor.

OR

  1. print out both of the 'Simple Spelling' pages listed on the side of this screen, and

  2. download a version of the letter by right clicking on this link, save it as a text file and edit it in your word processor, then send the letter -- with Simple Spelling pages attached -- to your favorite newspaper or magazine editor.

Letter to Editor

May 2, 2001



Editor's Name, and Title
Periodical or Company Name
Address
City, State and Zip


Dear [Editor's Name],

I'm an avid reader of your publication. I'm also an avid fan of literacy. Your readership base, of course, is directly influenced by the literacy rate in [appropriate city, state or region].

To a much lesser degree, the literacy rate in [ " ] is influenced by the editorial values and practices of people such as yourself.  For better or worse, the editorial choices you make have some bearing on local literacy rates.  Some literacy advocates suggest making texts simpler and plainer at the expense of accuracy.  Not me.  I ask that you consider a plain and simple idea that is also perfectly accurate and subtle: incorporate the authorized simpler spellings contained in the enclosed short lists of dictionary variants - several of which very likely grace the pages of [name of periodical] already.

According to mainstream dictionaries, variant spellings are by definition very nearly identical in frequency to their sister spellings.  As you will see, analysts at the American Literacy Council have underscored the simpler of the two, and are working with style manual and spell-check producers to simplify their preferred spellings list.

I hope to hear back from you regarding the extent of your decision to influence literacy for the better--and simpler--in this area.

Very cordially,


[your name]
