Wuns upon a tiem thair wer foer litl Rabits, and thair naems wer—

Flopsy,
Mopsy,
Coton-tael,
and Peeter.

Thay livd with thair Muther in a sand-bank, underneeth th root of a verry big fer-tree.

'Now mi dears,' sed oeld Mrs. Rabit wun morning, 'u may go into th feelds or doun th laen, but don't go into Mr. McGregor's garden: yur Faather had an acsident thair; he was puut in a pi bi Mrs. McGregor.'

'Now run along, and don't get into mischif. I am going out.'

Then oeld Mrs. Rabit tuuk a basket and her umbrela, and went thru th wuud to th baeker's. She baut a loef of broun bred and fiev curant buns.

Flopsy, Mopsy, and Coton-tael, hoo wer guud litl bunnies, went doun th laen to gather blackberries:

But Peeter, hoo was verry nauty, ran straet away to Mr. McGregor's garden, and sqeezd under th gaet!

Ferst he aet sum lettuces and sum French beens; and then he aet sum radishes;

And then, feeling rather sik, he went to luuk for sum parsly.

But round th end of a cuecumber fraem, hoom shuud he meet but Mr. McGregor!

Mr. McGregor was on his hands and nees planting out yung cabbages, but he jumpt up and ran after Peeter, waeving a raek and calling out, 'Stop theef!'

Peeter was moest dredfuly frietend; he rusht all oever th garden, for he had forgoten th way bak to th gaet.

He lost wun of his shoos amung th cabbages, and th uther shoo amungst th potaetoes.

After loozing them, he ran on foer legs and went faster, so that I think he miet hav got away alltogether if he had not unforchunetly run into a goosberry net, and got caut bi th larj butons on his jaket. It was a bloo jaket with bras butons, qiet nue.

Peeter gaev himself up for lost, and shed big teers; but his sobs wer oeverherd bi sum frendly sparroes, hoo floo to him in graet exsietment, and implord him to exert himself.

Mr. McGregor caem up with a siv, which he intended to pop upon th top of Peeter; but Peeter wriggled out just in tiem, leeving his jaket behiend him.

And rusht into th tool-shed, and jumpt into a can. It wuud hav bin a buetiful thing to hied in, if it had not had so much wauter in it.

Mr. McGregor was qiet shur that Peeter was sumwhair in th tool-shed, perhaps hiden underneeth a flower-pot. He began to tern them oever cairfuly, luuking under eech.

Prezently Peeter sneezd—'Kertyschoo!' Mr. McGregor was after him in no tiem.

And tried to puut his fuut upon Peeter, hoo jumpt out of a windo, upseting three plants. Th windo was too small for Mr. McGregor, and he was tierd of runing after Peeter. He went bak to his werk.

Peeter sat doun to rest; he was out of breth and trembling with friet, and he had not th leest iedeea which way to go. Allso he was verry damp with siting in that can.

After a tiem he began to waander about, going lippity—lippity—not verry fast, and luuking all round.

He found a dor in a wall; but it was lokt, and thair was no room for a fat litl rabit to sqeez underneeth.

An oeld mous was runing in and out oever th stoen dorstep, carrying pees and beens to her family in th wuud. Peeter askt her th way to th gaet, but she had such a larj pee in her mouth that she cuud not anser. She oenly shuuk her hed at him. Peeter began to cri.

Then he tried to fiend his way straet across th garden, but he becaem mor and mor puzld. Prezently, he caem to a pond whair Mr. McGregor fild his wauter-cans. A whiet cat was stairing at sum goeld-fish, she sat verry, verry stil, but now and then th tip of her tael twicht as if it wer aliev. Peeter thaut it best to go away without speeking to her; he had herd about cats frum his cuzin, litl Benjamin Buny.

He went bak tords th tool-shed, but sudenly, qiet cloez to him, he herd th noiz of a ho—scr-r-ritch, scrach, scrach, scritch. Peeter scuttered underneeth th buushes. But prezently, as nuthing hapend, he caem out, and cliemd upon a wheelbarro and peeped oever. Th ferst thing he saw was Mr. McGregor hoeing unyons. His bak was ternd tords Peeter, and beyond him was th gaet!

Peeter got doun verry qieetly off th wheelbarro; and started runing as fast as he cuud go, along a straet wauk behiend sum blak-curant buushes.

Mr. McGregor caut siet of him at th corner, but Peeter did not cair. He slipt underneeth th gaet, and was saef at last in th wuud outsied th garden.

Mr. McGregor hung up th litl jaket and th shoos for a scair-cro to frieten th blakberds.

Peeter never stopt runing or luukt behiend him til he got hoem to th big fer-tree.

He was so tierd that he flopt doun upon th nies sofft sand on th flor of th rabit-hoel and shut his ies. His muther was bizy cuuking; she wunderd whut he had dun with his cloeths. It was th second litl jaket and pair of shoos that Peeter had lost in a fortniet!

I am sorry to say that Peeter was not verry wel during th eevning.

His muther puut him to bed, and maed sum camomile tee; and she gaev a does of it to Peeter!

'Wun taebl-spoonful to be taeken at bed-tiem.'

But Flopsy, Mopsy, and Coton-tael had bred and milk and blackberries for super.
