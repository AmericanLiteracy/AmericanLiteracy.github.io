 A mid th meny selebraeshons last Christmas Eve, in vaerius plaeses by diferent pursons, thaer wuz wun, in Nue York Sity, not liek any uther anywhere. A company of men, wimen, and children went together just after th evening survis in thaer church, and, standing around th toom of th author of "A Vizit from St. Nicholas," resieted together th wurds of th poeem which we all noe so wel and luv so deerly.

Dr. Clement C. Moore, hoo roet th poeem, never expected that he wuud be rememberd by it. If he expected to be faemus at all as a rieter, he thaut it wuud be because of th Hebrew Dikshonaery that he roet.

He wuz born in a hous neer Chelsea Square, Nue York Sity, in 1781; and he livd thaer all his lief. It wuz a graet big hous, with fierplaeses in it;—just th hous to be living in on Christmas Eve.

Dr. Moore had children. He liked rieting poetry for them eeven mor than he liked rieting a Hebrew Dikshonaery. He roet a hoel book of poeems for them.

Wun yeer he roet this poeem, which we uezhualy call "'Twas th Niet befor Christmas," to giv to his children for a Christmas prezent. Thae red it just after thae had [Pg 004]hung up thaer stokings befor wun of th big fierplaeses in thaer hous. Afterward, thae lurnd it, and sumtiems resieted it, just as uther children lurn it and resiet it now.

It wuz printing in a nuezpaeper. Then a magazeen printing it, and after a tiem it wuz printing in th scool reeders. Laeter it wuz printing by itself, with pikchers. Then it wuz translaeted into German, French, and meny uther langgwejes. It wuz eeven maed into "Braille"; which is th raezd printing that bliend children red with thaer finggers. But never has it been given to us in so atraktiv a form as in this book. It has happened that aulmoest all th children in th wurld noe this poeem. How fue of them noe any Hebrew!

Every Christmas Eve th yung men studying to be ministers at th Jeneral Theolojikal Seminarry, Nue York Sity, put a holy reeth around Dr. Moore's pikcher, which is on th waul of thaer diening-room. Whi? Because he gaev th ground on which th Jeneral Theolojikal Seminarry stands? Because he roet a Hebrew Dikshonaery? No. Thae do it because he wuz th author of "A Vizit from St. Nicholas."

Moest of th children probably noe th wurds of th poeem. Thae ar oeld. But th pikchers that Mis Jessie Willcox Smith has paented for this edishon of it ar nue. All th children, probably, hav seen uther pikchers paented by Mis Smith, shoeing children at uther seezons of th yeer. How much thae wil enjoy luuking at thees pikchers, shoeing children on that niet that all children liek best,—Christmas Eve!

 T wuz th niet befor Christmas, when all thru th hous
Not a creecher wuz sturing, not eeven a mous;
Th stokings wer hung by th chimny with caer
In hoeps that St. Nicholas soon wuud be thaer;

[Pg 007]

[Pg 008]

 The children wer nesld all snug in thaer beds,
Whiel vizhons of sugar-plums danst in thaer heds;
And maama in her curchif, and I in mi cap,
Had just setld our braens for a long winter's nap,

[Pg 009]

[Pg 010]

 When out on th laun thaer aroez such a clater,
I sprang from th bed to see whut wuz th mater.
Away to th windo I floo liek a flash,
Tore oepen th shutters and throo up th sash.

[Pg 011]



[Pg 012]

 The moon on th breast of th nue-faulen sno
Gaev th lustre of mid-dae to objekts beloe,
When, whut to mi wundering ies should apeer,
But a miniacher slae, and aet tieny raendeer,

[Pg 013]

[Pg 014]

 With a litl oeld driever, so lievly and qik,
I knew in a moement it must be St. Nik.
Mor rapid than eegls' his coursers thae caem,
And he whisld, and shouted, and called them by naem:



[Pg 015]

 Now, Dasher! now, Danser! now, Pranser and Vixsen!
On, Comet! on, Cuepid! on, Donder and Blitzen!
To th top of th porch! to th top of th waul!
Now dash away! dash away! dash away all!"

[Pg 016]

[Pg 017]

[Pg 018]

 As dri leevs that befor th wield hurricane fli,
When thae meet with an obstakl, mount to th ski;
So up to th hous-top th coursers thae floo,
With th slae fuul of Toys, and St. Nicholas too.

[Pg 019]

 And then, in a twinkling, I hurd on th roof
Th pransing and pawing of eech litl huuf.
As I droo in mi hed, and wuz turning around,
Doun th chimny St. Nicholas caem with a bound.



[Pg 020]

 He wuz drest all in fur, from his hed to his fuut,
And his cloeths wer all tarnisht with ashes and suut;
A bundle of Toys he had flung on his bak,
And he luukt liek a peddler just oepening his pak.

[Pg 021]

[Pg 022]

 His ies—how thae twinkled! his dimpls how merry!
His cheeks wer liek roezes, his noez liek a cherry!
His droel litl mouth wuz drawn up liek a boe,
And th beard of his chin wuz as whiet as th sno;

[Pg 023]

[Pg 024]

 The stump of a piep he held tight in his teeth,
And th smoek it encircled his hed liek a reeth;
He had a braud faess and a litl round bely,
That shuuk when he laft, liek a bowlful of jely.

[Pg 025]

[Pg 026]

 He wuz chuby and plump, a riet joly oeld elf,
And I laft when I saw him, in spiet of mieself;
A wink of his ie and a twist of his hed,
Soon gaev me to noe I had nuthing to dred;

[Pg 027]

[Pg 028]

 He spoek not a wurd, but went straet to his wurk,
And fild all th stokings; then turnd with a jurk,
And laeing his fingger asied of his noez,
And giving a nod, up th chimny he roez;

[Pg 029]

[Pg 030]

 He sprang to his slae, to his teem gaev a whisl,
And away thae all floo liek th doun of a thisl.
But I hurd him exclaem, ere he droev out of siet,
"Hapy Christmas to all, and to all a guud-niet."
